How will I get a job without going to a bootcamp?
The idea that your bootcamp will get you a job is in most cases a myth. From the hundred people I know it took on average 3-5 months after graduating to happen. Bootcamp networks cannot sustainably land jobs for the exodus of junior programmers they pop out. Needing a job to make it as a software engineer is another myth (just look at me), but in any case companies hire programmers based on what they can actually do for the company.
Contract/freelance work builds your professional portfolio and is a great way to make money until you are proficient enough for a full-time job (if that is what you want). I will teach you how to find, manage, and bug-fix this work. For example, start a project slowly; progress is fastest at the beginning and clients will complain when they see this progress slow down as the application becomes more advanced. Then, bill the client for fewer hours to offset the fact that you're being paid to learn.
